# System Updates & Investigation Notes

## Issue 1: Why 500 Stocks Generated 0 Recommendations
During the recent 500-stock analysis scan, **0 recommendations** were generated because the `technical_score` for all stocks defaulted to its mathematical floor (`-0.500`). 

**Root Cause:**
The bottleneck resides in the **Enhanced Volume Confirmation** logic (`utils/enhanced_volume_confirmation.py`) which processes every "Buy" signal generated by the core mathematical strategies. 

To validate a Buy signal, the system takes an `np.mean()` (average) of several volume indicators (OBV, volume strength, divergence). Due to how these indicators are scored (e.g. "normal" volume adds `0.3`), the resulting average almost never reaches the strict required threshold of `>= 0.7`. This mathematically filters out almost 100% of signals, reverting them back to `0` (HOLD).

**Proposed Fixes for Later:**
1. **Reduce Threshold:** Lower the required volume confidence average from `>= 0.7` to `>= 0.45` to allow strong trades to pass.
2. **Change Logic:** Instead of taking an average, take the `max()` score. If any single volume signal is extremely powerful, the trade is validated.
3. **Toggle:** Temporarily set `ENABLE_VOLUME_FILTER = False` in `config.py` to see raw strategy performance.

## Issue 2: Flaws with using 1-Year CAGR for Swing Trading
Right now, the system uses an annualized return (CAGR) inside `scripts/backtesting_runner.py` to grade algorithms over historic data. However, evaluating a short-term swing trading strategy with a 1-year CAGR is fundamentally flawed for these reasons:

1. **Idle Cash vs Capital Rotation:** CAGR assumes capital is invested and compounding for 365 continuous days. In swing trading, capital is often rotated in 3–15 day bursts, and consciously sidelined into cash during bearish regimes. CAGR fails to accurately reflect the speed of rotation or the safety of cash holding.
2. **Artificial Magnification:** Annualizing a short, 5-day trade that yields 8% mathematically breaks the formula, projecting absurd and confusing numbers (e.g., 10,000%+ returns).
3. **Strategy Misalignment:** The primary objective of swing trading is capturing short-term burst momentum, not surviving a 1-year macro hold cycle. 

**Recommended Backtesting Metrics to Implement Later:**
Instead of looking at CAGR to grade an algorithm's performance, the backtesting module should be refactored to prioritize:
* **Win Rate / Strike Rate:** The percentage of trades hitting profit targets vs stop losses.
* **Average Holding Period Return (HPR):** Absolute percentage gained or lost *per trade*.
* **Average Trade Duration:** How many days capital is exposed in the market (e.g., 4 days on average).
* **Expectancy per Trade:** `(Win Rate × Avg Win) - (Loss Rate × Avg Loss)`. This guarantees profitability over 100 trades.
* **Maximum Drawdown:** Essential to ensure the algorithm doesn't recommend holding through flash crashes.
